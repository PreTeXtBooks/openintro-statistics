Definition 8.1.4. Correlation.

The correlation coefficient, denoted by \(r\text{,}\) describes the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two numerical variables. The correlation is calculated as:
\begin{equation*} r = \frac{1}{n-1}\sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{x_i - \bar{x}}{s_x} \cdot \frac{y_i - \bar{y}}{s_y} \end{equation*}
where \(s_x\) and \(s_y\) are the sample standard deviations of \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) respectively.
Properties of correlation:
  • The correlation is always between -1 and 1: \(-1 \leq r \leq 1\)
  • \(r = -1\) indicates a perfect negative linear relationship
  • \(r = 0\) indicates no linear relationship
  • \(r = 1\) indicates a perfect positive linear relationship
  • Correlation does not imply causation
  • Correlation is sensitive to outliers
  • Correlation measures only linear relationships
