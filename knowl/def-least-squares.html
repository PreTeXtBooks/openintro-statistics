Definition 8.2.1. Least Squares Regression Line.

The least squares regression line is the line \(\hat{y} = b_0 + b_1 x\) that minimizes:
\begin{equation*} \sum_{i=1}^{n} e_i^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2 \end{equation*}
The coefficients that accomplish this are:
\begin{align*} b_1 \amp = r \frac{s_y}{s_x}\\ b_0 \amp = \bar{y} - b_1 \bar{x} \end{align*}
where \(r\) is the correlation, \(s_x\) and \(s_y\) are the sample standard deviations, and \(\bar{x}\) and \(\bar{y}\) are the sample means of \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) respectively.
