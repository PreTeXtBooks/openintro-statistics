Linear regression is the statistical method for fitting a line to data where the relationship between two variables, \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) can be modeled by a straight line with some error:
\begin{equation*} y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x + \varepsilon \end{equation*}
where:
  • \(\beta_0\) and \(\beta_1\) are the modelâ€™s parameters
  • \(\varepsilon\) represents the error (epsilon)
  • \(x\) is the explanatory variable or predictor
  • \(y\) is the response variable
The parameters are estimated using data, and we write their point estimates as \(b_0\) and \(b_1\text{.}\) The fitted line is written as:
\begin{equation*} \hat{y} = b_0 + b_1 x \end{equation*}
